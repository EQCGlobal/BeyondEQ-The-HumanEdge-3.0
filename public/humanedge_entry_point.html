Stage 4 — The HumanEdge Framework
Six levels of
emotional maturity
Not a hierarchy of better or worse people. A map of how human capability develops — from unconscious reaction to invisible influence. Most people operate between Levels 2 and 4. Few reach 5. Fewer still reach 6.
Where are you on this map?
This experience gives you a directional sense. The 360 diagnostic — where the people in your life rate your observable behaviors — gives you the real answer. The gap between how you see yourself and how others experience you is the most important data the 360 produces.
Stage 5 — Your 360 Observer Guide
Choosing the right
observers
The 360 is only as reliable as the observers you invite. This guide helps you select the right people — across the right relationship types — to produce meaningful data.
The four observer categories
Above
Invite 1–2 people
People who hold authority over you. They observe how you operate under scrutiny and accountability pressure.
  • Has observed you across multiple situations over at least 3 months
  • Has seen you under pressure — deadlines, conflict, difficult decisions
  • Will give an honest rating even if it is uncomfortable
  • Someone you've worked with for less than 6 weeks
  • Someone who only sees you in formal, prepared contexts
Peer
Invite 2–3 people
People at a similar level who work alongside you. They observe how you operate without power differential.
  • Works with you regularly — at least weekly interaction
  • Has experienced conflict or disagreement with you
  • Knows you well enough to notice when you're off your game
  • A close friend who primarily knows you socially
  • Someone who wants to please you or fears your reaction
Below
Invite 1–2 people
People who report to you or work under your direction. The most important and most overlooked category. They observe how your capability holds — or drops — when you hold the power.
  • Has experienced you under pressure — when you're stressed or conflicted
  • Trusts that their honest feedback is genuinely safe to give
  • Has seen how you handle mistakes — yours and theirs
  • Someone who feels their position depends on your approval
  • Someone you've recently had significant conflict with
Personal
Invite 1 person (optional)
Someone who knows you outside the professional context. Optional but powerful — they see behaviors professional observers never access.
  • Knows you across multiple life contexts
  • Will be honest without fear of damaging the relationship
  • Has experienced conflict or difficulty with you
  • Someone currently in significant conflict with you
  • Someone who has never seen you under real pressure
What to tell your observers
"I am completing a development assessment and I need your honest observations — not your support. The most useful thing you can do is rate what you actually observe, not what you think I would want to hear. Your identity will not be shared with me — only your observer category."

What comes next — the 360 diagnostic
Your observers will rate your behaviors across 15 skills using 68 observable statements. You will rate yourself on the same statements. The gap between self and observer ratings — particularly across different relationship types — produces the real EMM level assessment and your development plan.
After the 360 — the facilitated debrief
A trained HumanEdge facilitator will walk you through your results. Not the numbers — the meaning. The patterns. The development path that is specific to you.
